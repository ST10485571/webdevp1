About Kamvelihle Harvest Foundation

Our Mission & Vision

The Kamvelihle Harvest Foundation is dedicated to combating food and clothing insecurity amoung South Africa's homeless and vulnerable populations by efficiently collecting and distributing quality donated surplus from local and global partners; fostering community; and restoring dignity through the spirit of Ubuntu.

Our vision is a South Africa where no person experiences the indignity of hunger or the hardship of inadequate clothing, and where communities flourish through shared resources and compassionate action.

Our Brief History

The Kamvelihle Harvest Foundation (KHF) was born in late 2022 from the stark reality of food waste amidst widespread hunger in South Africa. What started as a small, volunteer-driven effort in Johannesburg quickly grew, fueled by the spirit of Ubuntu. By mid-2023, KHF formalized as an NPC and secured PBO status, attracting partnerships with major supermarkets like Shoprite and Pick n Pay. The past year has seen accelerated growth, with collaborations extending to retail brands like PEP Stores and Woolworths for clothing, and crucial endorsements from South African celebrities such as Nomzamo Mbatha and Siya Kolisi. This support, combined with significant investment from philanthropists and corporate foundations, has enabled us to expand our reach, establishing key "Harvest Hubs" in major urban centers.

Our Programs in Action

Harvest Hubs: Food Rescue Program

Our "Harvest Hubs" are the heart of our food rescue efforts. We partner with local and global supermarket chains to collect surplus, near-expiry, or aesthetically imperfect yet perfectly edible food items. This includes fresh produce, baked goods, and packaged foods that would otherwise go to waste.

Our dedicated teams ensure safe collection, rigorous quality checks, and careful sorting. The food is then packaged into nutritious meal kits or bulk supplies, ready for distribution to those in need. This program not only feeds hungry individuals but also significantly reduces food waste in South Africa.

Dignity Threads: Clothing & Essentials

Through our "Dignity Threads" initiative, we provide essential clothing and hygiene items to the homeless. We collaborate with leading local retail brands to receive donations of unsold stock, factory seconds, or returned items.

This program ensures that individuals not only receive necessary items but also experience the dignity that comes with appropriate and clean attire.

  • Preparing and packaging nutritious meals in our Harvest Hubs.
  • Sorting and organizing donated clothing and essentials.

Donate & Support Our Cause

Your generosity fuels our mission. Every contribution, big or small, directly impacts the lives of those we serve.

Monetary Donations:

Financial contributions allow us the flexibility to purchase essential items, cover operational costs, and expand our reach. As a registered Public Benefit Organisation (PBO); all monetary donations to Kamvelihle Harvest Foundation are tax-deductible in South Africa (Section 18A certificates available upon request).

Food & Clothing Donations:

We gratefully accept donations of non-perishable food items, fresh produce (from commercial partners), and new or gently used clothing and blankets.

How to Donate In-Kind:

  • **Drop-off at Harvest Hubs:** Visit our main Harvest Hubs in Johannesburg, Cape Town, or Durban during operating hours (details in Contact section).
  • **Partner Retail Stores:** Look for designated Ubuntu Harvest Foundation collection bins at participating Shoprite, Pick n Pay, PEP Stores, and Woolworths branches nationwide.
  • **Organize a Drive:** Contact us if you'd like to organize a large-scale food or clothing drive in your community or workplace.

*Please ensure all clothing donations are clean and in good condition. For food, we prioritize non-perishable items, but commercial partners can donate surplus fresh produce directly.*
